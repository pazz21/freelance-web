Business Process Management

BRVS Group follows a well-defined project execution methodology. BRVS Group's management has laid down organizational policy guidelines towards the development of the organization's processes. BRVS Group's quality processes are developed and maintained by a dedicated Quality Processes Group (QPG), drives BRVS Group's project execution methodology.

The QPG defines SDLC processes, Procedures, Manuals, Guidelines, and Formats and Templates to be used for projects. In addition SEPG also maintains the Process Database of published results, artifacts of past projects, library of reusable components and training materials. The Project Managers and the Software Engineering Team are adequately trained in the use of OSSP for their projects.

The BRVS Group Methodology addresses various requirements from Project Acquisition, through Project Execution to Quality Assurance.

The Project's defined Software Processes are derived from the QPG and tailored to suit a specific situation. The project processes could be derived for various types of projects namely: Software Development projects, Product Implementation projects, Migration projects, Customization projects, and Maintenance & Support projects.

BRVS Group's business process is designed with the following objectives:

  • Understand the client's requirements correctly and completely - Design and develop the solution with the client's needs in mind
  • Execute the project in close coordination with the client
  • Ensure that all the deliverables are on-time and within budget
  • Detect and fix defects at an early stage of the project
  • Ensure all quality objectives of the project are met
  • Provide adequate training to users, ensuring a high comfort level before transition
  • Provide post-implementation support to ensure that the client takes charge of the system.

A technically strong Software Quality Assurance group involves itself from the very beginning itself, e.g. from the Proposal stage of a project, through the completion of the project.

Application Integration

Our vision for Enterprise Application Integration is the creation of a real-time, 24 x 7 eco-system, which ties together a company's employees, its partners and customers. Business processes are supported by business applications, which in turn use shared business application services, tied together by integration middleware. Business services are more generic in nature and can be shared among applications. Examples of shared services are content management, user profiling and user security. Integration services allow the various application and services to communicate.

Integration of business applications is driven by an integration framework, which defines the cross-application process workflow in a flexible multi-tiered object-oriented (delegating) architecture and takes proactive action in response to significant events in the workflow.

Distributed systems and "publish and subscribe" messaging technologies are particularly effective in marshalling worldwide forces within and outside the company to address exceptional events in the basic workflow. BRVS Group produces event-driven architectures that exploit publish and subscribe systems, and encourage distribution of resources.
